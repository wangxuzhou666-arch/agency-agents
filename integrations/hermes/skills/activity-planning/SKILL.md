# Activity Planning (活动策划)

## Overview

Activity planning is the strategic design and execution of promotional campaigns, contests, giveaways, and interactive events on Xiaohongshu to drive engagement, acquire followers, generate user-generated content, and achieve specific marketing objectives through time-bound activities.

## When to Use

**Use when**:
- Planning contests, giveaways, or sweepstakes
- Organizing holiday or seasonal promotional campaigns
- Creating hashtag challenges or participation activities
- Coordinating milestone celebrations or brand events
- Designing interactive campaigns to boost engagement
- Launching new products with promotional activities
- Building community through participatory events

**Do NOT use when**:
- Creating regular content calendar (use content-calendar)
- Running ongoing marketing without time-bound activity (use content-marketing)
- Planning paid advertising campaigns (use advertising)
- Organizing offline events without online component (use event-marketing)

## Core Pattern

**Before** (passive posting, no engagement tactics):
```
❌ "Just posting content, hoping people engage"
❌ "No urgency or incentive to participate"
❌ "One-way communication, no interaction"
❌ "Missed opportunity to create buzz and FOMO"
```

**After** (strategic activities, engagement explosions):
```
✅ "7-day giveaway: 1,200 entries, 3,000+ new followers"
✅ "Hashtag challenge: 5,000+ user posts, massive UGC"
✅ "Contest generates 50K engagements in 48 hours"
✅ "Time-bound activity creates urgency and FOMO"
```

**5 Activity Types**:
1. **Giveaways** - Free products or prizes for participation
2. **Contests** - Skill or creativity-based competitions
3. **Challenges** - Hashtag-driven participation campaigns
4. **Interactive Events** - Live sessions, AMAs, workshops
5. **Milestone Celebrations** - Brand anniversaries, follower celebrations

## Quick Reference

| Activity Type | Engagement | Follower Growth | UGC Generated | Complexity | Best For |
|---------------|------------|-----------------|---------------|------------|----------|
| **Giveaway** | Very High | High | Low | Low | Quick follower boost, product awareness |
| **Contest** | High | Medium | High | Medium | Quality content, brand alignment |
| **Challenge** | Very High | High | Very High | Medium-High | Viral potential, community building |
| **Interactive Event** | Medium | Medium | Low | Low | Community engagement, relationship building |
| **Milestone** | High | Low-Medium | Medium | Medium | Brand loyalty, community celebration |

## Implementation

### Step 1: Define Activity Objectives

**Clarify Activity Goals**:
```
Primary Objectives (Choose 1-2):

Follower Growth:
- Target: Acquire X new followers from activity
- Best achieved with: Giveaways, challenges
- Typical growth: 500-5,000 followers depending on prize and promotion

Engagement Boost:
- Target: Achieve X engagements (likes, comments, shares)
- Best achieved with: Contests, interactive events
- Typical lift: 3-10x above normal engagement rate

User-Generated Content:
- Target: Generate X user posts with hashtag
- Best achieved with: Challenges, contests
- Typical UGC: 100-5,000 posts depending on incentive

Product Awareness:
- Target: Introduce product to X potential customers
- Best achieved with: Giveaways, sampling activities
- Typical reach: 10,000-100,000 impressions

Community Building:
- Target: Strengthen relationship with existing followers
- Best achieved with: Interactive events, milestone celebrations
- Typical participation: 5-15% of follower base

Sales Conversion:
- Target: Drive X purchases or leads
- Best achieved with: Giveaways with purchase entry, flash sale events
- Typical conversion: 2-8% entry-to-purchase rate

Success Metrics:
- Quantitative: Follower growth, engagement count, UGC volume, reach
- Qualitative: Sentiment, brand perception, community feedback
- Cost-per-result: Total investment / metrics achieved
- ROI: Revenue or value generated / investment
```

**Choose Activity Type**:
```

Type 1: Giveaway (Simplest, Highest Participation)
Best For: Follower growth, product awareness

Structure:

- Winner selection: Random drawing

✅ Easy to enter (low friction)
✅ High participation rates
✅ Quick follower boost
✅ Simple to execute

Cons:
❌ Attracts freebie seekers (low quality followers)
❌ Minimal brand engagement
❌ Little UGC created
❌ Followers may unfollow after
```

### Step 2: Design Activity Mechanics

**Entry Requirements**:
```

Keep it Simple (The Rule of 3):
Maximum 3 entry steps to maximize participation

Optimal Entry Structure:
Step 1: Follow account (mandatory, builds follower base)
Step 2: Engage with content (like, comment, share)
Step 3: Amplify reach (tag friends, share to story)

Entry Examples by Complexity:

Simple (Highest participation):
"1. Follow us
2. Like this post
3. Comment your favorite product"

Moderate (Good balance):
"1. Follow us
2. Like and share this post
3. Tag 3 friends"

Complex (Lower participation, higher quality):
"1. Follow us
2. Post photo with hashtag #BrandNameChallenge
3. Tell us your story (50+ words)
4. Tag us and 3 friends"

Bonus Entries (Encourages amplification):
- Share to story: +2 entries
- Post about us: +5 entries
- Tag multiple friends: +1 entry per friend (up to 5)
- Use branded hashtag: +3 entries
- Subscribe to newsletter: +10 entries

Entry Method Options:
Option 1: Comment-based entries
- Easy to track
- Public (creates social proof)
- Best for: Giveaways, simple contests

Option 2: Hashtag-based entries
- Generates UGC
- Creates searchable content
- Best for: Challenges, photo contests

Option 3: Form-based entries
- Collects data (email, demographics)
- Private (less social proof)
- Best for: Lead generation, detailed submissions

Option 4: DM-based entries
- Personal interaction
- Higher engagement quality
- Best for: Interactive events, exclusive activities
```

**Prize Structure**:
```

Prize Budget Allocation:
Total Budget: ¥X

For Giveaways (High quantity, lower value):
- 1 grand prize: 30-40% of budget
- 5-10 runner-up prizes: 40-50% of budget
- Participation prizes: 10-20% of budget

For Contests (Lower quantity, higher value):
- 1 grand prize: 60-70% of budget
- 2-3 runner-up prizes: 20-30% of budget
- Honorable mentions: 5-10% of budget

For Challenges:
- 1 grand prize: 50% of budget
- Daily spotlights (non-monetary): recognition
- Participation rewards: 10% (samples, discount codes)

Prize Selection Criteria:
1. Relevance to Brand
   ✅ Your products or services (ideal)
   ✅ Complementary products from partners
   ✅ Experiences aligned with brand values
   ❌ Random unrelated prizes (attracts wrong audience)

2. Perceived Value
   - Should feel generous, not cheap
   - Perceived value > actual cost (wholesale vs retail)
   - Example: ¥899 retail value product (¥300 cost to you)

3. Prize Mix:
   - Grand prize: High value, creates excitement (¥500-2,000)
   - Runner-up: Medium value, more winners (¥100-300)
   - Participation: Low value, many winners (¥10-50 or samples)

Prize Presentation:
- Use retail value, not cost: "Worth ¥899!" not "Costs us ¥300"
- Bundle products for higher perceived value
- Add experiences (consultation, event) to increase value
- Show product images, not just descriptions

Example Prize Structures:

Giveaway Prize Structure:
🎁 Grand Prize (1 winner):
Complete skincare set + ¥200 gift card = ¥1,099 value

🎁 Runner-Up Prizes (5 winners):
Star product bundle = ¥399 value each

🎁 Participation Prizes (50 winners):
20% off code + free sample = ¥50 value each

Total retail value: ¥4,998
Total cost to brand: ~¥1,500
Reach: Potentially thousands of entries

Contest Prize Structure:
🏆 Grand Prize (1 winner):
¥2,000 shopping spree + feature on brand account

🏅 Runner-Up (3 winners):
¥500 product bundles

🥉 Honorable Mention (10 winners):
¥100 gift cards

Total value: ¥5,500
Entries expected: 100-500 high-quality submissions
```

**Timing and Duration**:
```

Optimal Duration by Activity Type:

Giveaway: 3-7 days
- Too short (<3 days): Not enough time for viral spread
- Too long (>7 days): Loses urgency, participation drops
- Sweet spot: 5-7 days for maximum entries

Contest: 7-21 days
- Too short (<7 days): Insufficient time for quality submissions
- Too long (>21 days): Participants procrastinate, lose interest
- Sweet spot: 14 days (2 weekends included)

Challenge: 7-14 days
- Needs to be short enough to maintain momentum
- Long enough for participation
- Sweet spot: 7-10 days

Interactive Event: 1-2 hours (live) or 3-7 days (async)
- Live: Respect attention spans, max 2 hours
- Asynchronous: Give enough time for participation

Milestone Celebration: 3-7 days
- Multiple activities spread across period
- Builds sustained engagement

Best Start Days:
- Monday-Tuesday: Build momentum during week
- Avoid: Friday evening (people disconnect), major holidays

Best Start Times:
- Morning: 7-9 AM (catch commuters, early risers)
- Evening: 7-9 PM (prime time, after dinner)
- Avoid: 9 AM-5 PM (people at work, lower engagement)

Duration Examples:
- Giveaway: "Monday 7 AM to Friday 11 PM" (5 days)
- Contest: "Monday 7 AM to Sunday 11 PM" (7 days, includes 2 weekends)
- Challenge: "Today only! 24-hour challenge" (urgency)
  OR "Week-long challenge, new prompts each day" (sustained)

End Times:
- End during high-engagement times (7-9 PM)
- Avoid: Sunday night (people dreading work week)
- Best: Thursday or Friday evening (weekend anticipation)
```

**Rules and Compliance**:
```

Essential Rules (Always Include):

Eligibility:
- Age requirement: "Must be 18+ or have parent permission"

- Employee exclusion: "Employees and families not eligible"

Entry Limits:
- "Limit one entry per person" (for fair contests)

Winner Selection:

- "Prize is non-transferable"
```

### Step 5: Manage Activity Execution

**Entry Tracking and Management**:
```

Tracking Methods:

Method 1: Manual Tracking (Small scale, <500 entries)
Tools: Spreadsheet, comments review
Process:
- Export or copy comments
- Verify entry requirements
- Count entries per participant
- Use random number generator for winner

Pros: Free, simple
Cons: Time-consuming, error-prone, doesn't scale

Method 2: Hashtag Tracking (UGC activities)
Tools: Native platform search, third-party tools
Process:
- Monitor hashtag for all posts
- Verify participants followed entry steps
- Engage with submissions
- Select winner from qualified entries

Pros: Creates UGC, searchable content
Cons: Public entries, harder to verify compliance

Method 3: Form-Based Tracking (Larger scale, 500+ entries)
Tools: Google Forms, Typeform, specialized tools
Process:
- Create form with entry fields
- Collect data (name, contact, entry proof)
- Automatically track entries
- Random drawing from submissions

Pros: Scalable, collects data, fair selection
Cons: Higher barrier to entry, tool cost

Method 4: Platform Tools or Third-Party Services
Tools: Xiaohongshu business features, contest platforms
Process:
- Use built-in or integrated tools
- Automate tracking and winner selection
- Compliance with platform rules
- Professional execution

Pros: Easiest, most professional
Cons: Cost, learning curve

Daily Management Checklist:
✅ Monitor entries and engage (like, reply to comments)
✅ Track entry count and growth
✅ Respond to questions and issues
✅ Remind people to enter (stories, comments)
✅ Share user posts (if UGC challenge)
✅ Address rule violations or fake accounts
✅ Update social proof stats
✅ Monitor sentiment and feedback
```

**Community Management**:
```

Engage with Participants:

Respond to Every Comment (First 48 hours):
- "Thanks for entering! Good luck! 🍀"
- "Love your enthusiasm! Have you tried our [product]?"
- "Great question! Here's the answer: [helpful response]"

As Volume Grows (48+ hours):
- Like every comment (shows engagement)
- Reply to questions only
- Heart/react to supportive comments
- Don't spam reply to every entry

Address Common Questions:
Create FAQ comment and pin it:
"Q: Is this open internationally?
A: Currently China only, sorry!

Q: When will winner be announced?
A: Winner will be notified by [date]!

Q: Can I enter multiple times?
A: Each comment = one entry, enter as much as you want!

Q: Is this real?
A: Absolutely! We've done [X] giveaways before,
 check our highlights for past winners!"

Handle Issues:

Fake Accounts or Bots:
- Monitor for suspicious activity
- Disqualify obvious fake accounts
- Don't publicly shame (just ignore)
- Report to platform if severe

Negative Comments:
- Don't delete legitimate criticism
- Respond professionally: "We hear you! Here's our perspective..."
- Move to DMs for complex issues
- Know when to disengage
```

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why Happens | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Entry requirements too complex | Want to maximize value | Keep to 3 steps max, prioritize simplicity |
| Prize not relevant to brand | Choose expensive over relevant | Offer your products or highly complementary ones |
| Duration too long or too short | No data on optimal timing | Giveaways: 5-7 days, Contests: 14 days |
| No reminders or promotion | Set and forget | Schedule 3 reminders, cross-promote |
| Not engaging with entries | Overwhelmed by volume | Like every comment, reply to questions |
| Winner selection not transparent | Rushed process | Document process, explain how winner chosen |
| No follow-up after winner announced | Move to next activity | Feature winner, share their content, thank all |
| Ignoring negative feedback | Fear of bad publicity | Address concerns professionally, learn from criticism |
| Not tracking or analyzing results | Miss the learning opportunity | Document metrics, conduct post-mortem |
| Violating platform rules | Didn't read guidelines | Study platform rules, comply fully |

## Real-World Impact

**Case Study: Successful Giveaway Campaign**

A skincare brand ran a 7-day giveaway to launch a new product line.

**Activity Details**:
- Prize: Complete skincare set (5 products) worth ¥899
- Entry: Follow, like, comment, tag 2 friends
- Duration: 7 days
- Promotion: 3 posts (announcement, 2 reminders), cross-platform

**Results**:
- Entries: 3,456 qualified entries
- Follower growth: +1,528 followers (from 8,000 to 9,528 = 19% growth)
- Engagement: 12,847 total engagements across giveaway posts
- Reach: 87,000 impressions
- Cost: ¥300 (product cost) + ¥200 (promotion) = ¥500 total investment

**Post-Activity Analysis** (30 days later):
- Follower retention: 1,236 still following (81% retention rate)
- Cost per acquired follower: ¥500 / 1,236 = ¥0.40 per follower
- Purchases from giveaway participants: 87 orders (5.6% conversion)
- Revenue from new followers: ¥15,660 (average order ¥180)
- ROAS: 31x (¥15,660 revenue / ¥500 investment)
- Winner became brand advocate, posted UGC, made 3 repeat purchases

**Improvements Implemented for Next Giveaway**:
- Reduced duration from 7 to 5 days (maintained momentum better)
- Added purchase bonus entry (+5 entries for purchase)
- Improved winner announcement timing (7 PM vs 11 AM)
- Added survey to gather feedback

**Next Giveaway Results** (with improvements):
- Entries: 4,123 (+19% increase)
- Follower growth: +1,891 (+24% increase)
- Conversion rate: 7.8% (+39% improvement)
- ROAS: 42x (+35% improvement)

**Data-Backed Insights**:
- Well-designed giveaways can deliver 15-25x ROAS
- Follower retention of 75-85% is achievable with relevant prizes
- 5-7% entry-to-purchase conversion rate is realistic
- Multi-post strategy (announce + 2 reminders) outperforms single post by 3x
- Story reminders increase entries by 40-60%
- Relevant product prizes outperform cash/gift cards by 2x on quality
- Giveaway participants have 2-3x higher lifetime value than organic followers
- 80% of participants will engage with brand within 30 days if nurtured
- Contests generate 3-5x more UGC than giveaways
- Challenges can go viral with 10,000+ user posts if trend-aligned
